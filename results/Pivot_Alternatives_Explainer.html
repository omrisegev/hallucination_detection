Pivot Alternatives Pilot — what we tried instead of L-SML, and why we're keeping it

Step 151 · branch experiment/pivot-alternatives · July 3, 2026 · all runs local CPU (~1 hour total), no GPU, no new inference — everything runs on already-cached data.

TL;DR. Because FUSE (Candès group, Apr 2026) uses the same unsupervised-fusion family we do, we stress-tested whether L-SML can be replaced by other unsupervised methods over the same entropy-trace signal. We piloted 10 alternative methods across two tracks with gates fixed before the runs. Both gates failed: the best alternative aggregator loses ~10pp to L-SML continuous on the 29-cell battery, and no temporal model beats even a sliding-window baseline on raw traces. The useful outcome: this is now evidence that our aggregation layer is not an interchangeable commodity — which strengthens, not weakens, the position that the thesis contribution is the entropy-trace signal with L-SML as a validated tool. Verdict: no pivot.

1. Why this branch exists

FUSE ("Ensembling Verifiers with Zero Labeled Data", Lee … Candès, arXiv 2604.18547) builds on the same Parisi/Jaffe SML lineage that our L-SML fusion comes from. Both Ofir and Bracha flagged it as the closest competing paper. The concern: if L-SML is "their" method too, what happens to our novelty? A Gemini research session produced five pivot candidates (docs/research_notes/thesis_pivot_options.md); this branch is the critical assessment of those candidates plus an empirical pilot of the ones that survived scrutiny.

One framing correction up front: the pivot doc mixes two different problems. (a) The FUSE-novelty question is about the aggregation layer — it can be answered by swapping the aggregator, nothing "online" required. (b) Online/streaming detection is a separate ambition that the Step-148 pilot already gated against (prefix L-SML beats a sliding window only in the earliest 10% of the trace). We tested the two separately: Track A for (a), Track B for (b).

3. What we actually ran: tasks, domains, data

Everything scores the same object: for each generated answer, the per-token Shannon entropy trace H(n) of the model's own next-token distribution (gray-box — log-probabilities only, no hidden states). The question is only how to turn that trace into one hallucination score per answer. Labels (final-answer correctness) touch nothing but the AUROC evaluation.

Track A — can anything replace L-SML as the aggregator? (29-cell battery)

Each cell = one (task, model) pair with cached per-answer 16 spectral/time-domain features of H(n) and correctness labels. The full battery:

4. The methods, in plain terms

Track A candidates — one score per answer from its 16-feature vector

All are classic unsupervised anomaly/density detectors, fit per cell on all unlabeled answers of that cell, scoring "how unusual is this answer's feature vector":

MethodHow it scores an answer
MahalanobisDistance from the cell's mean, whitened by a shrunk covariance (Ledoit–Wolf). The linear-Gaussian baseline.
GMM (k=2)Negative log-likelihood under a 2-component Gaussian mixture — can model a bimodal "correct/incorrect" population.
KDENegative log-likelihood under a kernel density estimate — fully non-parametric density.
Isolation ForestHow few random splits isolate the point — tree-based, no density assumption.
Autoencoder (AE)Tiny network (16→8→4→8→16) learns to compress-and-reconstruct the cell; score = reconstruction error. 5 seeds averaged.
PRAE-style robust AESame AE, but each sample gets a learned stochastic gate that can exclude it from training (target: keep 80%) — the AE shapes its manifold on suspected inliers only. In the spirit of Lindenbaum, Aizenbud & Kluger (ICML 2021).

Track B candidates — one score per answer from its token-by-token trace

MethodHow it scores an answer
2-state Gaussian HMMEM learns a "calm" and a "high-entropy" regime + switching probabilities from all traces, unsupervised (states identified by entropy mean — labels never touch the fit). Score = fraction of the answer spent in the high-entropy regime. This is the honest version of the IMM proposal.
BOCPDBayesian online change-point detection on H(t): tracks the probability at each token that the local statistics just shifted. Score = expected number of change points in the trace.
AR(2) / Kalman innovationsFit a cheap predictor of the next entropy value; score = how badly the trace resists prediction (mean squared innovation). This is the KalmanNet go/no-go: if innovations from a fixed filter carry no label signal, a learned filter gain has nothing to amplify.

Kept honest by construction

  • Same information access as L-SML. L-SML continuous estimates its fusion weights from the unlabeled covariance of the very cell it scores. Every candidate gets exactly the same: features of the cell, zero labels. Nobody sees a label before evaluation.
  • Label-free score orientation, three tiers. An anomaly score doesn't know which direction means "wrong". We report: raw (fixed convention: more anomalous = hallucination), anchored (sign matched to the oriented mean-entropy view — the label-free device from Step 148; this tier gates), and oracle (best of both directions using labels — diagnostic only, but it is the tier directly comparable to how the stored L-SML numbers were computed, so our gate is conservative).
  • Gates fixed before the runs. Gate A: candidate macro AUROC within 1pp of L-SML continuous on common cells = viable hedge. Gate B: beat the best DeepConf sliding window AND full-trace L-SML, with a paired-bootstrap 95% CI excluding zero.
  • Sanity anchors. Recomputed Track-B baselines match the stored Step-148 values to 4 decimal places (max diff 0.0000). Smoke tests with planted outliers / planted regime switches pass for every method.

Reading. Three diagnostics pin down why this family fails:

  • PRAE ≤ plain AE (−1–2pp): the robust gating adds nothing here.
  • PRAE ≈ Mahalanobis (+1pp): the nonlinearity adds essentially nothing either.
  • Only reasoning shows life (GMM 0.701 — still ~8pp under L-SML's reasoning macro); GPQA and RAG sit at chance for every scorer.

The structural explanation: anomaly/density scorers are direction-free — they flag distance from the bulk in any direction of feature space. But the label-relevant structure in our features is a specific oriented consensus direction (each feature has a known sign; correct answers sit on the agreeing side). Estimating that consensus direction from unlabeled data is exactly what L-SML's eigenvector step does, and it is exactly the information a density model throws away.

Reading.

  • KalmanNet: NO-GO. Innovations do carry label signal (0.70–0.72) — but at ρ = 0.93–0.97 with plain mean entropy they are the level signal repackaged, and they sit below the trivial baselines. A learned Kalman gain would have to overturn this from 200 unlabeled traces; there is no headroom evidence.
  • No "hallucination momentum". The pivot doc's premise is that hallucination is a sticky Markov regime. The fitted HMM says otherwise: the high-entropy state's self-transition is 0.46 (vs 0.77 for the calm state) — high-entropy visits are short bursts, not a persistent mode.
  • The one real finding: BOCPD is orthogonal. Every other candidate re-measures the entropy level. BOCPD's change-point count is uncorrelated with it (ρ ≈ 0) yet alone scores 0.685–0.703 — a genuinely new signal our 16 features don't encode.

8. Conclusions

  1. No pivot. Ten alternative methods, two pre-registered gates, both failed with margin. L-SML continuous over the spectral entropy-trace features remains the best unsupervised method we have tested — now also against 6 anomaly scorers and 4 temporal-model families, on top of the earlier U-PCR / flat-SML / simple-average comparisons.
  2. The FUSE answer improves. The established defense (contribution = the signal; FUSE innovates on the fusion side of the same pipeline and validates the family) now has an empirical leg: the aggregation layer is not swappable at zero cost — consensus-direction fusion measurably matters. Choosing L-SML is a decision with teeth, not a borrowed convenience.
  3. Dropped for cause: KalmanNet (innovations = repackaged entropy level), LOCA (no bursts), IMM (its honest HMM version failed), flows-on-hidden-states (white-box, off-signal), the hybrid stack (unvalidated composition).
  4. One live thread: BOCPD change-point count as an extra fusion view — level-orthogonal, free to re-check on the next raw-trace data. No dedicated resources.
  5. For the thesis: this branch is a ready-made "alternatives considered" section with pre-registered gates and honest nulls (memo: docs/research_notes/thesis_pivot_assessment.md).
