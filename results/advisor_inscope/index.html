Headline

GOOD_6 is the leading label-free detector at 75.94% macro AUROC, beating the GOOD_5 reference by +0.75 pp (Wilcoxon p = 0.00507, 18 wins / 7 losses over 25 cells). No label-free feature-selection method we tested beats it. The best of them — the new pseudo-label-gated selector (a6.pl_dufs, Step 194) at 75.24% — is the first to nominally edge GOOD_5 (+0.05 pp, Wilcoxon p = 0.173, not significant); the previous best, the Gated-Laplacian (a2.dufs) at 75.02%, sits just below GOOD_5.

With labels, logistic regression over the same views reaches 78.10%, so the supervised headroom is +2.16 pp. That gap is the honest measure of how much our detector leaves on the table.

Two corrections to previously circulated numbers. A data-integrity audit found that one cell's bench rows, and every learned-selector row on 11 further cells, had been computed against a superseded feature cache and were never recomputed. Both are fixed here. The GOOD_6-over-GOOD_5 margin moves from +0.98 pp to +0.75 pp, and the best learned selector moves from marginally ahead of GOOD_5 to marginally behind it.
