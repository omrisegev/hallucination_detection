The trace-based Gated-Laplacian selector — per-feature stochastic gates trained on a Laplacian-trace objective over the gated sub-matrix — is the independence-aware label-free selector most directly relevant to this work. It was raised as the strongest option we had not yet built.

It was already built and already benchmarked. It is implemented in spectral_utils/selectors/a2_groupfs.py as the per-feature gate rule, and ships as bench variant a2.dufs. It has rows on all 25 in-scope cells. The objective is the Laplacian trace on the gated sub-matrix plus a gate-sparsity penalty; the gate regularisation strength is chosen label-free by selection stability across seeds, which is our own addition rather than the source method's prescription.

What the per-feature rule actually changes

Compared with the group-granular rule (a2.select, macro 74.81%), the per-feature rule is a clean structural improvement in one respect and a null result in another:

  • It eliminates gate saturation entirely — from 12 of 25 cells selecting the whole pool down to none. That isolates saturation as a consequence of group granularity rather than of the gates themselves, which is a clean and quotable finding.
  • It does not convert that into accuracy. Macro moves +0.21 pp, the difference from GOOD_5 is not significant, and on the worst cell it remains catastrophic.
Recommendation withheld on building a further variant. The motivation for a new gate variant was to fix saturation. Saturation turns out to be already solved by the per-feature rule, and solving it did not help. With the honest selection headroom at +1.74 pp over GOOD_5 and two thirds of any apparent gain being winner's curse, there is little room for a new label-free selector to win, and no measured mechanism left to attack.

Grounding status. The source method is currently cited second-hand through the group-discovery paper's description of it; the primary paper is not yet in papers/. The implementation should be read as “our reconstruction of the method as described by its successor” until that is closed.
