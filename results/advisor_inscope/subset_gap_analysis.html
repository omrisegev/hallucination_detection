The open question after the last round was: on cells where a searched subset looks better than our fixed one, why does no label-free selector deliver that gain? There are two very different answers, and they call for opposite responses — either the search is failing and we should build better selectors, or the gain was never real and we should stop.

The split-half oracle settles it. A subset is chosen greedily on half the data and then scored on the held-out half:

The answer is mostly “there was nothing to find”. Searching subsets beats GOOD_5 on every single cell in-sample, but two thirds of that advantage evaporates out of sample, and on 6 of 25 cells the searched subset is actually worse than the fixed one. The remaining honest headroom over GOOD_5 is +1.74 pp — and GOOD_6 already captures a large part of it.
