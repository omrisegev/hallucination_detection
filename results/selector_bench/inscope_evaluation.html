Honest ceiling — how much is really on the table?

The label-peeking exhaustive-sweep oracle is a winner's-curse artifact (Step 189). This is the out-of-sample counterpart: bounded greedy forward selection on a held-out half, refit and scored on the other half, R=10 random 50/50 splits per cell. It answers "if selection could see labels on half the data, how much would it honestly gain?"

  • QA (10 cells): GOOD_5 0.7222 → greedy@H16 0.7145 → greedy@30v 0.7256
  • math (15 cells): GOOD_5 0.7687 → greedy@H16 0.7812 → greedy@30v 0.7945
QA honest ladder (wide pool): 0.7200 → 0.7256 (+0.55pp). Math: 0.7692 → 0.7945 (+2.53pp). The wide-pool greedy ceiling sits close to GOOD_5 — consistent with the leaderboard's learned-selector ties. The achievable prize from in-cell label-free selection is small; the reliable win is the curated GOOD_6.
