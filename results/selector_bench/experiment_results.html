Headline: the 46-view pool (19 replication-grid cells)

The deployment-shaped arm: give the algorithm the raw, uncurated canonical pool and no labels. GroupFS (a2.select) matches the label-derived fixed subsets — 0.7323 vs GOOD_5 0.7328 and top_macro_5 0.7364 — choosing a different subset in almost every cell (12 distinct subsets / 19 cells, sizes 9–28). Only GOOD_6 (0.7440) still leads it. A4's variants (purple, from the uncommitted Antigravity worktree, repgrid-19 rows only) tie GOOD_5 when keeping GOOD_5's features and swapping K, and trail when actually selecting.

The curated 16-feature pool (51 cells) — where selection does NOT help

On H16 every learned selector loses to the fixed references: the pool is already the product of curation, so there is little left to select away and mistakes are expensive. The best learned selector here is A4's anchor-affinity family — but it is indistinguishable from its own anchor: epr alone scores 0.6606, a4.anchor_s4 0.6593. The per-cell oracle (0.7472, +7.6pp over GOOD_5) remains uncaptured by every family.

Reading the whole experiment

  • Dynamic label-free selection reaches fixed label-derived quality on the raw pool (a2.select ≈ GOOD_5/top_macro_5), with zero fallbacks — the intended deployment win. One robustness fix (the coqa cell) would put it strictly ahead of both.
  • GOOD_6 is still the best fixed subset (+1.2pp over a2.select); the label-free ceiling has not caught the best curated set.
  • Clustering / K is not a lever: every swap variant lands within ±0.6pp of its untouched counterpart.
  • Fit-statistic objectives are refuted (admissibility), reconstruction objectives are refuted twice (A3 = A4-CSSP), and anchor-affinity collapses to epr alone.
  • The per-cell oracle prize (+7.6pp, concentrated on RAG/GPQA) is untouched by all six families — see the benchmark page for what that means going forward.
  • The feature audit reframes pool curation: no pool feature is flat noise; 13/30 are informative but carried with the wrong fixed sign on this grid (L-SML absorbs that via negative weights) — see the feature-value-audit page.
