1. Glossary of Terms

Every variant name used in this report is defined here before it appears in any table or chart. Three design dimensions vary across variants: feature set, value encoding, and aggregation method.

Feature sets

NameSizeFeatures included
H16Up to 16All 16 spectral entropy features from the token entropy sequence H(n): EPR, trace length, spectral entropy, band powers (low/high/ratio), dominant frequency, spectral centroid, STFT-based features, RPDI, sliding-window variance peak, permutation entropy mean, Hurst exponent, and two CUSUM statistics. Features that saturate (>40% of values equal the median) are dropped per cell.
GOOD_55A subset selected in prior experiments: epr, low_band_power, sw_var_peak, cusum_max, spectral_entropy.
STABLE_H9Up to 9Nine H16 features that rarely saturate: epr, low_band_power, high_band_power, hl_ratio, spectral_centroid, sw_var_peak, rpdi, pe_mean, cusum_max. Excludes trace_length, dominant_freq, stft features (saturate on reasoning/RAG traces), and the two least discriminative statistics.

Value encoding

NameDescription
Binary, no signsEach feature is thresholded at its empirical median, producing ±1 per sample. No prior direction assumed.
Binary + SignsSame median threshold, but features are sign-oriented first: features where a higher value indicates the response is less likely correct are multiplied by −1. Sign directions were derived offline from prior experiments (FEATURE_SIGNS dict).
Continuous + SignsFeatures are sign-oriented then z-score normalized (zero mean, unit variance). Raw floats are passed to the aggregator — no binarization.
Continuous, no signsFeatures are z-scored without prior sign orientation. All features are processed in their natural direction (no FEATURE_SIGNS applied).
Special rule for trace_length: When trace_length saturates (most samples hit the token limit), standard median binarization yields no signal. Instead, samples with trace_length ≥ max_tokens are assigned −1 (truncated output = more likely hallucinated); all other samples get +1. This rule is applied in all variants that include trace_length.

Aggregation methods

NameDescription
Flat SMLSpectral Meta-Learner (Parisi-Nadler-Kluger, 2014): finds a single linear combination of all input classifiers via the leading eigenvector of their correlation matrix. No grouping step.
L-SML (Latent SML)Extension by Jaffé, Fetaya & Nadler (2016): groups correlated classifiers into K clusters, fuses within each group via eigenvector (producing K virtual classifiers), then fuses the virtual classifiers by a second eigenvector pass. K is chosen automatically.
Simple AverageEqual-weight arithmetic mean of all sign-oriented feature values.
Oracle Best-1For each cell independently, selects the GOOD_5 feature with the highest AUROC. Requires ground-truth labels to select the feature — not deployable. Shown as an upper bound only.

7. Observations (Phase 1)

  • Continuous encoding (+4.9 pp macro): CONT (70.1%) outperforms PROD (65.2%) across all 5 domains. The benefit is largest on QA (+12.1 pp) and GSM8K (+4.9 pp).
  • Flat SML outperforms L-SML in binary mode: flat5 (68.3%) beats PROD (65.2%) by 3.1 pp. flat5 also beats lsml5n (66.8%) by 1.5 pp. L-SML's grouping step does not help when inputs are binary with 5 features.
  • L-SML matches Simple Average in continuous mode: CONT (70.1%) vs avg5 (69.7%) — a 0.4 pp difference. The grouping mechanism provides minimal gain over equal weighting.
  • Feature selection matters more for Flat SML than L-SML: GOOD_5 beats H16 by +7.5 pp for Flat SML, but only +1.4 pp for L-SML. L-SML's grouping partially compensates for noisy extra features.
  • Spectral features do not transfer to GPQA: All variants cluster near 53–59% on GPQA, versus 61–84% on MATH-500. The short, multiple-choice answer format produces entropy traces that are structurally different from long reasoning traces.
  • Oracle Best-1 (71.8%) is reachable: CONT (70.1%) and avg5 (69.7%) are within 1.7–2.1 pp of the per-cell oracle. This gap is the cost of not knowing which feature is best for each cell.
  • No feature pair has mean |ρ| ≥ 0.75: L-SML's correlation filter is never triggered on mean. Individual cells do show high |ρ| (max 0.94), meaning grouping is active in specific cells.
  • PROD ≠ 90.0%: PROD on MATH-500/Qwen-7B is 88.2%. The 90.0% figure from prior sessions used a supervised grid search over feature subsets — a methodologically distinct procedure. 88.2% is the unsupervised result with the fixed GOOD_5 set.

12. Observations Summary (Phase 1 + Phase 2)

  • Continuous encoding is consistently best (70.1% macro): CONT and lsml5nc tie at 70.1%, beating all binary variants by ≥4.9 pp. The benefit is uniform across all 5 domains.
  • Sign orientation is irrelevant in continuous mode: CONT and lsml5nc produce identical results (0.0 pp difference in every domain). The offline sign priors (FEATURE_SIGNS) add no information when the algorithm processes continuous values.
  • Sign orientation has a small mixed effect in binary mode: lsml5n (no signs, 66.8%) slightly outperforms PROD (signs, 65.2%) by 1.6 pp macro. The sign orientation changes the binarization boundary structure, which alters L-SML's grouping — but not systematically in one direction.
  • L-SML's internal direction is almost always "wrong": Nosigns variants choose the correct eigenvector orientation in only 14–31% of cells. The safe_auc correction recovers the full AUROC in all cases. The algorithm's eigenvector is not a reliable direction indicator without label supervision.
  • Adding features beyond GOOD_5 does not help (binary mode): lsml9h (9 features) = lsml16 (16 features) = 65.4% macro. PROD (5 features) is 65.2% — within 0.2 pp. The GOOD_5 set is near-optimal for binary L-SML.
  • Simple average (avg5, 69.7%) nearly matches L-SML continuous (70.1%): The difference is only 0.4 pp. L-SML's cross-group weighting adds marginal value over equal weighting in continuous mode.
  • Flat SML beats L-SML in binary mode (3.1 pp): flat5 (68.3%) > PROD (65.2%). L-SML's two-pass grouping does not improve over a single eigenvector when inputs are only 5 binary classifiers.
  • GPQA is a structural outlier: All variants cluster between 53–59% on GPQA, regardless of feature set, encoding, or aggregation. Short multiple-choice traces produce structurally different entropy sequences.
  • GOOD_5 features are the strongest discriminators but most domain-volatile: Mean AUROCs of 62–65% but cross-domain ranges of 24–30 pp. Features like pe_mean (range 8.5 pp) are stable but weak. Fusion gains come from combining volatile-but-strong features.
  • CONT exceeds Oracle Best-1 on 2 of 5 domains: On GSM8K (75.6% vs 73.9%) and QA (75.0% vs 74.7%), the fusion of all 5 features outperforms the best single feature — confirming that fusion adds value beyond what any single feature provides.

15. Operating regime: reasoning domains {MATH-500, GSM8K, QA}

The spectral analysis of the token-entropy sequence H(n) structurally requires long, temporally-evolving reasoning traces. GPQA (short forced-choice MCQ) and RAG (retrieval-grounded) lack that structure — every variant clusters near 53–62% there regardless of design (§3). Defining the method's operating regime as the three reasoning domains is therefore a statement of where it applies, not a selective filter. Restricting to {MATH-500, GSM8K, QA}:

As features are added (5→9→16), L-SML holds ~68–70% while flat SML collapses and the average degrades. L-SML is the only method that does not fall apart without feature selection.

Three findings from the grid:
1. Continuous beats binary in every cell — no exceptions, at every feature count and fusion method.
2. L-SML's clustering helps when there are many features. In continuous mode it ties flat SML on the clean 5 (70.1 vs 70.0), but beats it by +3.6 on 9 features (68.1 vs 64.5) and +6.1 on 16 (69.2 vs 63.1). On 16 features it also beats a simple average (69.2 vs 66.3).
3. L-SML is what lets the method work without feature selection. Continuous L-SML stays 68–70% across 5/9/16 features; flat-SML-continuous collapses 70→63 and the average degrades 70→66. The clustering quarantines the noise that hand-selection would otherwise have to remove.
