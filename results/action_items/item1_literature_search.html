Item 1 — Literature search: successors to the 2016 spectral meta-learner

Question from the meeting: did the spectral meta-learner line (Jaffe-Fetaya-Nadler 2016) continue after 2016, and is anything in that line directly usable? Answered in HISTORY Steps 139–141 and folded into the advisor email; recap below.

U-PCR — the direct continuation, and effectively what we already run

U-PCR (Tenzer et al. 2022, AISTATS) is the continuous-input successor in the Jaffe-Fetaya-Nadler line: it drops the binary-vote requirement and estimates ensemble weights from the continuous score covariance under an uncorrelated-error assumption. Our L-SML continuous pipeline is essentially this estimator; since Step 160 we score both L-SML and U-PCR on every replication-grid cell (results/repgrid/scores_lsml_upcr.csv), and this session extended U-PCR to all legacy local caches (results/subset_sweep/upcr_legacy.csv — see the per-domain breakdown).

FUSE — the closest competing paper (positioning)

FUSE (Candès et al., April 2026) sits in the same theoretical lineage but differs on the two axes that define our contribution: signal (FUSE ensembles external verifier models; we use the internal per-token entropy trace of the generating model itself, single pass, no extra models) and task (FUSE selects among N candidate answers, Best-of-N; we score one answer for hallucination risk). Both advisors flagged it independently; the differentiation memo is in Research_Directions.md §Item 1. The contribution to defend is the signal, not the fusion algebra.

Deep L-SML

A deep extension (RBM-equivalence) of the same estimator family. Relevant only if the feature set grows well past ~16 views and the pairwise |ρ| ≥ 0.75 correlation filter starts rejecting subsets wholesale; parked, no action.

Bottom line: the line continued (U-PCR, Deep L-SML), we already run its continuous form, and the nearest competitor (FUSE) is distinguishable on signal and task. Sources: Research_Directions.md §Item 1, HISTORY Steps 139–141.
