Where the gap lives

The macro gap is not uniform across domains: ≈0pp on reasoning (GSM8K, MATH-500 — both fusions sit near the in-sample ceiling there, so the features, not the fusion, are the bottleneck), +4.9pp on GPQA and +5.8pp on RAG+QA (where labels let LR re-weight features whose usefulness varies per cell). LR and L-SML weight vectors correlate only weakly (Spearman 0.1–0.2) — supervision is buying different weights, not just better scaling.

Bottom line: label-free fusion gives up 3.6–4.7pp macro vs a supervised LR on identical features, and essentially nothing on the reasoning domain that is the thesis headline. The gap is a QA/GPQA phenomenon.
