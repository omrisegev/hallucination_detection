Item 4 — Benchmarking against published detectors

How this was built

For each competitor paper we run our own inference on the paper's exact (dataset, model, N, decoding config), so the accuracy and label distribution match their setup. We then score our L-SML / U-PCR offline on that trace — we never re-implement their detector — and place our AUROC next to their published number. This inference-only, offline-scoring boundary is documented in cluster/reasoning_grid_runbook.md and BENCHMARKING_COMPETITOR_GUIDE.md. Every cell passes the gate ladder local smoke test → N=30 pilot → full N; numbers land in results/reasoning_benchmark.csv and results/repgrid/scores_lsml_upcr.csv.

Gate policy (formalized 2026-07-12, applies desk-wide — reasoning and QA). Two tiers. (1) Band violation — cell accuracy outside [0.20, 0.85] — is a quality flag: the cell is scored, appears in every table and figure with a CEILING / FLOOR tag, and is excluded from the headline win tally. A flagged number is a noisy estimate of the right quantity. (2) Label-validity failure — truncation-label leakage (cap-pinned negatives) or a single-class label set — is a documented REJECT, never scored: there the AUROC would be a clean estimate of the wrong quantity, and no caveat fixes that. Current REJECTs: ars_gsm8k_qwen3_8b (15/29 negatives cap-pinned at 8192), ars_math500_qwen3_8b (23/50 negatives cap-pinned at 16384 — Qwen3's reasoning on hard MATH-500 items is effectively unbounded), noise_gsm8k_gemma2b (acc 0.000, single class). The Qwen3/ARS pair is closed as REJECT-leakage — itself a reportable finding about capped greedy Qwen3 protocols.
