The "peak" at T=1.5 is confounded by the accuracy collapse (80% → 4%) — the class mix shifts under the curve, and T=2.0 has only 8 correct answers. Gate G-T1 FAIL (overlapping CIs at 27.5% accuracy). So Q1's honest answer: no usable temperature lever here.

Q2 (primary) — diversity vs simply more passes

Paired on the 200 common samples: condition A (K=5 passes, all T=1.0) vs condition B (K=5 passes at T = 0.3/0.6/1.0/1.5/2.0).

Paired deltas: A − base = +6.1pp [+0.4, +12.8] (more same-T passes help); B − A = −5.3pp [−10.3, −1.1] (CI excludes 0 — temperature diversity hurts). Gate G-T2 FAIL, sign negative.

Mechanism (off-diagonal cross-pass correlation): same-T passes correlate at ρ ≈ +0.45 — same signal plus independent noise, so averaging denoises. Multi-T passes correlate at ρ ≈ +0.01 — but that decorrelation is the off-temperature passes being near-random (T=0.3/0.6 weak, T=2.0 degenerate), not independent true signal.

Answer to the meeting question: the multi-pass lift is variance reduction from repeated sampling at a single good temperature (T≈1.0); mixing temperatures dilutes it. Temperature is not the lever; repeated sampling is.

Two caveats surfaced (never followed up)

(1) spectral_entropy's sign is temperature-dependent (AUROC 0.26 @ T=1.0 vs 0.14 @ T=1.5 under the fixed −1 sign — informative if flipped). (2) The label-free fusion underperforms its best single feature at every T (fused 85.1 vs cusum_max 92.7 @ T=1.0) because the epr anchor weakens at low T — so Q1's low-T dip is plausibly a fusion/anchor artifact, not a signal property. The Step-0b anchor test this session (scrutiny point 4) addressed the anchor's sign robustness on the full battery; its low-T strength remains follow-up #3 below.

Independent later confirmation

The Step-168 Internal-States rerun at T=0.8 (after the T=1.0 sampling-collapse confound was caught) independently confirms the core caveat — decoding temperature strongly confounds accuracy/label mix — without changing any Phase-15 number.
