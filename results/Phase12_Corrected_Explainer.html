Phase 12 Corrected — The Benchmarking Story (Step 152)

July 4, 2026 · Colab A100 run, fresh inference at T=1.0 · notebook: notebooks/Spectral_Analysis_Phase12_Corrected.ipynb · results dict: Drive cache/phase12_corrected/phase12_corrected_results.pkl

TL;DR. On the domain the thesis lives in — reasoning traces — the 1-forward-pass spectral method (L-SML over the token-entropy trace) beats every K=5/K=10 sampling baseline computed on the exact same answers: GSM8K 0.754 vs 0.701 for the best competitor. But the run also delivered three uncomfortable findings that block a citable comparison table for now: (1) most competitor baselines collapse on the fresh shared caches relative to the old Phase 12 numbers (drops of 16–25pp); (2) our MATH-500 score came out globally sign-flipped (a known eigenvector ambiguity — fixable, but the orientation-fixed value still disagrees with the old-cache reference); (3) SelfCheckGPT lands below chance on all four RAG datasets. The sampling-fusion experiment (Item 5) answered its question cleanly: 10 extra samples add ≈nothing on top of the 1-pass signal.

1. Why we ran this

The thesis needs a competitor table we can defend in front of a reviewer. The old Phase 12 table couldn't be defended, for two reasons:

  • The baseline implementations were not what the papers report. Our "semantic entropy" was the discrete, count-only variant (cluster the K samples with an NLI model, take entropy of cluster counts) — the papers' ablation, not their headline estimator, which weights clusters by sequence likelihood. Our SelfCheckGPT took a hard argmax over NLI labels per sentence–sample pair; the official method uses the soft contradiction probability. Step 145 caught both.
  • Every number came from a different cache. Different question subsets, different sampling configs, different sessions. Methods were never compared on the same answers.

So this run re-did everything on one fresh shared cache per cell: one main pass at T=1.0 (which gives our entropy trace) plus K=10 samples of the same questions (which feed every sampling baseline). Three full-battery cells — GSM8K/Llama-3.1-8B (n=200), MATH-500/Qwen2.5-Math-7B (n=200, MAX_NEW=2048), GPQA-Diamond/Qwen2.5-7B (n=150) — plus four SelfCheckGPT-only RAG cells (L-CiteEval, ~50 questions each). This closes advisor Item 4 (benchmark completion) and answers Item 5 (does sampling add anything on top of the 1-pass signal?).

3. The domain we care about: reasoning traces (GSM8K)

The thesis claim has always been scoped: spectral features of the entropy trace work on reasoning-heavy generation, where the trace is long enough to carry structure. GSM8K/Llama-3.1-8B is the anchor cell — and this is the third independent run landing at 0.754–0.760, so the number is stable.

4. Competitors, before and after: the collapse

The uncomfortable headline of the run is not our number — it's the baselines. On the fresh shared caches, with paper-accurate scoring available, most old Phase 12 competitor numbers fall apart:

Two changes are confounded here. The corrected runs changed (a) the method implementations and (b) the inference caches (new questions, new samples, longer generations: MAX_NEW 1024/2048). We cannot yet attribute the drops to either alone. The prime suspect for the semantic-entropy drops is the NLI cross-encoder's ~512-token input truncation: on this run's long traces the clustering model literally does not see most of the answer. Two observations support "the harness is fine, the drops are real and specific": MATH-500 self-consistency — which needs no NLI model — is stable (0.872 → 0.863), and the D-SE/LW-SE pair (same clusters, different weighting) agree with each other to 0.1–0.5pp everywhere. Until the drops are reconciled, neither the old nor the new competitor table is citable.

5. The macro picture: all methods × all cells

Per-cell reading, honestly:

  • GSM8K — the clean win. See §3.
  • MATH-500 — self-consistency wins the cell at 0.863 [0.80, 0.91]: on math with an extractable final answer, majority vote over 10 samples is simply a strong method. Our score is sign-flipped (§6); even orientation-fixed (0.770) it would sit below SC here. Both SE variants collapse to ≈0.63 despite SC proving the samples themselves carry signal — which points the finger at the NLI stage, not the cache.
  • GPQA — nobody can do it. All sampling baselines 0.50–0.51, VC below chance at 0.428 (the model's self-ratings are anti-correlated with correctness), L-SML best at 0.553 [0.45, 0.66] — weak, but the only method above chance on the cell.
  • RAG ×4 — SelfCheckGPT lands below chance on all four datasets, and the corrected official variant is consistently worse than the hard variant (e.g. hotpotqa 0.243 [0.14, 0.36] — significantly anti-predictive). Old Phase 12 gave 51–57% here. Something is inverted or mis-graded in the long-context RAG setting; investigation required before any of these numbers mean anything.

6. The MATH-500 sign flip — what happened and why it's fixable

L-SML's fused score is the leading eigenvector of a correlation matrix between feature views. Eigenvectors are defined up to a global sign: v and −v are the same solution, so which one the solver returns is a coin flip. A flipped run produces the exact mirror score — AUROC 0.230 instead of 0.770. This is a known failure mode: Step 148 hit it in the streaming pilot and added a label-free fix (anchor_orient: orient the fused score to correlate positively with an anchor feature whose direction we know from domain knowledge). This notebook was written before that fix existed and calls the pipeline without it.

The flip is the explained part. The gap is not. Orientation-fixed, MATH-500 lands at ≈0.770 — far below the 0.944 this cell scored on the old T=1.0 cache (Step 135). The runs differ in traces (fresh sampling, MAX_NEW 2048 vs 1024) and question subset. Until that discrepancy is understood, neither 0.944 nor 0.770 should be cited for MATH-500.

7. Item 5 — is sampling orthogonal to the 1-pass signal?

The experiment: add LW-SE as a 6th view inside the L-SML fusion and check two pre-registered conditions — ρ(L-SML, LW-SE) < 0.75 (signals not redundant) and fused > best single method + 1pp (fusion actually helps).

Verdict: gate NOT passed — and that's the useful answer. Buying 10 extra forward passes plus an NLI model adds ≈nothing on top of the 1-pass spectral score. Read in the other direction, the same numbers make the thesis point: on GSM8K, the spectral trace adds +14.5pp on top of LW-SE. The orthogonality is real, but the information flows from the entropy trace to the sampling methods — not the other way.
