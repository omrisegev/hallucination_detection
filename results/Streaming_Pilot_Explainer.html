1. What were we trying to do

Everything we have built so far answers a post-hoc question: given a finished answer, was it hallucinated? The pilot tests a pivot to the streaming question:

Can we detect that a long chain-of-thought is going wrong while it is still being generated — unsupervised, from the per-token entropy trace H(n) alone?

Concretely: compute our 16 spectral/time-domain features on growing prefixes of the entropy trace (first 16, 32, 64, … tokens), fuse them per budget with continuous L-SML, and measure how detection AUROC evolves as the trace unfolds. If most of the full-trace signal is already present early, the method becomes an online monitor: flag (or abort) doomed traces mid-generation and save the wasted tokens.

Four experiments, with pass/fail gates fixed before running:

ExpQuestion
E1Prefix-AUROC curves — how early does detection quality saturate?
E2Baseline shoot-out at every budget — does the spectral suite beat a naive windowed statistic?
E3True online monitor — causal per-token risk score, threshold sweep → detection rate vs false alarms, and how early the flag fires
E4Early-exit value — at 5%/10% false alarms, what fraction of wasted tokens does aborting save?

Labels (final-answer correctness) are used only for evaluation, never inside the method.

2. The paper that does this before us

TitleStreaming Hallucination Detection in Long Chain-of-Thought Reasoning
ReferencearXiv:2601.02170
PublishedarXiv preprint, January 2026. No peer-reviewed venue as of July 2026 — preprint only.
Authors / groupsLu, Pan, Li, Nan, Zhuang, Zhao, Sun, Wang, Liu — Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications, Nanyang Technological University, Southwest Jiaotong University, Renmin University of China

They pose essentially our target task: monitor a long CoT trajectory step by step and decide, at each point, whether the reasoning has entered a hallucinated state ("identifying 78% of CoT instances as reasoning unfolds"). This is the closest prior work and the reason the pilot exists — before committing the thesis to this direction we needed to know where we stand relative to them.

How they evaluate

  • Data: a custom long-CoT dataset built from MuSiQue and other challenging benchmarks — 10,000+ trajectories, 200k+ annotated steps, generated by three models: LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-8B.
  • Two granularities: step-level ("does this reasoning step introduce incorrect/unsupported information?") and prefix-level ("has the reasoning prefix entered a hallucinated state?" — the setting closest to our prefix-AUROC).
  • Metrics: AUC / Accuracy / F1, plus a battery of dynamic streaming metrics (detection lag, immediate capture rate, etc.).
  • Their baselines: TTPD, SAPLMA, ICR Probe, LLM-Check, global-mean aggregation of hidden states.

The second reference point: DeepConf

Deep Think with Confidence (arXiv:2508.15260, August 2025) is not a detection paper — it uses lowest group confidence (the worst sliding-window mean of token confidence) to early-stop and filter reasoning traces for test-time efficiency. It matters to us for two reasons: (a) it is black-box like us, and (b) its core statistic is computable on our cached entropy traces, so it is the only competitor we can reproduce under identical conditions. It was therefore the primary success bar (gate G2): if our spectral suite cannot beat a windowed entropy mean, the fusion machinery adds nothing in the streaming regime.

3. What the pilot found

Cells: GSM8K/Llama-3.1-8B (n=200, clean) and MATH-500/Qwen-1.5B (n=400, clean but a weaker non-canonical run) for the gates; two GPQA/DeepSeek-R1 cells excluded from gates because 99–100% of their traces were truncated at a 1024-token cap mid-<think>.

The one replicated positive: paired bootstrap on stored scores shows the only statistically significant spectral edge over DeepConf sits in the earliest 10% of the trace — on both clean cells (+9.8pp [+2.3,+17.1] GSM8K, +4.6pp [+0.6,+8.7] MATH-500). The spectral fusion helps exactly where a sliding-window statistic is starved of tokens. At longer budgets the two converge.

Protocol caveats — this is context, not a head-to-head. Different benchmark (their custom MuSiQue-derived long-CoT set vs our GSM8K/MATH-500), different labels (their Claude-4.5 step annotations vs our final-answer correctness), different trace lengths. The legitimate talking point: on the same model family (Llama-3.1-8B), an unsupervised signal computed from output logprobs only lands at the level of their supervised hidden-state probe — in a setting their own limitations section says they cannot operate in (black-box / API-only).

Online monitor (E3/E4)

A causal per-token risk score (best: running-max entropy) flags 38% of eventually-wrong GSM8K traces at 10% false alarms; aborting at the flag saves 28% of all tokens spent on wrong traces. MATH-500: 28% detected, 8% saved. Modest but real early-exit economics from a zero-cost signal.

4. Next steps for this direction

  1. Regenerate raw traces (one Colab run — the blocking item). The pilot exposed data debt: MATH-500/Qwen-7B — our strongest cell (~90% full-trace AUROC) — has no raw trace cache anywhere (the Phase-12 K=10 files saved texts only), and no clean DeepSeek-R1 cell exists (all R1 traces were capped at 1024 tokens mid-reasoning). One inference run: MATH-500/Qwen-7B + an R1 cell with a ≥4096-token cap, saving token_entropies + top-50 logprobs per the raw-data rule.
  2. Replicate the earliest-prefix edge on the regenerated cells. The +5–10pp early-budget win is the pilot's one significant finding; it must replicate on stronger cells, with absolute budgets (the honest online setting — fraction budgets need oracle trace length) and enough samples for the paired test.
  3. If it replicates, reframe the contribution as a hybrid early-warning monitor. Spectral fusion where windows starve (first ~10% of the trace), windowed confidence thereafter — rather than claiming the fusion wins everywhere, which G2 refutes.
  4. Stabilize per-budget fusion. Refusing L-SML at every budget re-rolls the eigenvector's global sign (fixed with an oriented-epr anchor this run), and the 16-feature variant stays budget-unstable even anchored. Cleanest candidate fix: fit fusion weights once at a reference budget offline and reuse them across budgets.
  5. Optional label upgrade. The pilot was deliberately label-free. A step-level annotation pass (LLM-annotated hallucination onset on our traces, mirroring their Claude-4.5 protocol) would enable detection-lag metrics and a fairer prefix-level comparison — decide after step 2, not before.
  6. Advisor decision. Present the pilot verdict honestly: early signal is real (G1), the pure "streaming pivot" as framed is not supported (G2 fail), the early-prefix niche is the surviving thread. Decide: pursue the hybrid framing, or fold streaming in as one section of the current thesis line.
